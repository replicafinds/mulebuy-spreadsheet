The Stone Island Naslan Light jacket is a step up in complexity from the hoodie — and a more visible piece where construction details matter more. Here is what to know before ordering.

What Makes the Naslan Light Distinctive

The Naslan Light fabric is a specific lightweight technical material with a distinctive sheen and drape. It is lighter than most technical shells and has a semi-gloss surface that catches light differently than standard nylon. Sellers who use the correct fabric produce a jacket that moves and sits correctly. Sellers who substitute a cheaper material produce something that looks approximately right in photos but feels wrong immediately in person.

The Compass Badge on a Jacket

On a jacket the compass badge sits on the left arm in the same position as on the hoodie, but the attachment method and backing are slightly different. The badge should sit flat against a technical fabric surface — slightly harder to achieve than on a cotton hoodie because the material does not grip the attachment stitching the same way.

Request a QC photo showing the badge from multiple angles: front-facing, side profile (showing attachment method), and with the sleeve held straight out. These three views reveal whether the badge sits correctly on the shell fabric.

Hardware Details

The Naslan Light jacket has YKK-compatible zippers with specific Stone Island pulls. The main zip, pocket zips, and any additional hardware should all match. Mismatched zipper hardware is a common shortcut on lower-quality versions.

Seasonal Colorways

The Naslan Light comes in seasonal colorways that change regularly. Black and navy are perennial. Seasonal colors (olive, dusty rose, steel blue) appear in limited quantities from sellers who track Stone Island's seasonal releases.
